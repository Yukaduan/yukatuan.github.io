🍑 How to Acquire Language Scientifically

  • ZH
  • EN

This article mainly introduces some practical methods to acquire foreign languages ​​scientifically. The reference book is Science of Foreign Language acquisition

  1. Focus on Single Area for Input
  • Pick materials that you are interested in
  • Repeatedly listen to the material that you can understand 80% of the content (understandable input)
  • Change simply “learning” foreign languages in​​to “using” foreign languages ​​to obtain information
    • For example, listen to the same news in your native language in the morning and a foreign language in the evening.
    • The brain is processing speech information (pronunciation, rhythm, etc.) even if the content is incomprehensible
  1. The Role of Reciting Example Sentences
  • Expand your database
  • recite dialogues, sentences, expressions
  • Cultivate basic social communication skills
  1. Insist on Making Some Output Every Day
  • Speak + Write
  • Keep a diary, record self-talk, talk with foreign language learning partners, participate in foreign language corners, comment on the input materials heard and read in foreign languages ​​in online chat.
  • Improve the brain’s processing speed when foreign language information is input.
  • Normalize foreign language practice
  • The first principle of speaking is to convey meaning. If you have more time and energy, pay attention to whether the sentence is correct and whether the pronunciation is standard
  • Striking a balance between correctness and fluency
  1. Communication Strategy
  • “Buy time”(use I think, besides… instead of em… uhh…) especially in your intermediate stages of language acquisition
  • Interpretation method. Choose another expression.
  1. Meaningless Learning and Meaningful Acquisition
  • Memorizing - meaningless learning - difficult
  • Illustration, storytelling, homophony, contextual memory
  1. Memorize Words in Context
  • When encountering words that you cannot understand, guess the meaning first, and then look up the dictionary
  • While remembering the meaning of the word, you can remember the collocation
    • Especially suitable for memorizing verbs
  • Acquire the connection information
  • recite derivatives with prefixes and suffixes
  1. Learning Pronunciation Shall Starts with Imitation
  • Consciously identify sounds that are difficult for you
  • For audio materials that you already understand the meaning and grammar, you can further imitate the pronunciation, rhythm, intonation, and use the “shadow practice method”
  • Interest for pronunciation. Choose the type of pronunciation you are interested in (e.g. actors you like)
  • Retelling interview material is better than film clips, because the lines in the film do not necessarily correspond to real life.
  • For native speakers, what matters is not the pronunciation of individual vowels and consonants, but the overall intonation and rhythm. (feature imitation)
  1. Master the Basic Grammar to Make Sentences
  • The learner only needs to master the basic grammar knowledge (about the third grade to the senior one level) from completely ignorant to being able to output. If you want to further understand the input content, it is better to master some slightly more complicated grammar knowledge.
  • If you encounter some complex grammars that you can’t even understand, you can ignore them completely, because the time investment is too expensive and the effect is poor.
  1. Improve Learning Motivation
  • Learning motivation is not good or bad, as long as you can enjoy it.
  • Increase the interest in foreign culture (virtuous circle).
  1. Find Your Own Learning Strategy
  • Characteristics of excellent foreign language learners:
    • Pay attention to language form (written or oral)
    • Emphasis on communication and willingness to use language
    • Actively participate in learning activities
    • Follow the learning process
    • Use learning strategies flexibly
  1. Input Mode Demonstration
  • Natural Pedagogy - Grammar can be reserved for after-school learning, and students should be given comprehensible input practice in class.
  • Input-interaction mode - simulate interviews with learned grammar, communicate around things around you, covering various sentence patterns and expressions.
